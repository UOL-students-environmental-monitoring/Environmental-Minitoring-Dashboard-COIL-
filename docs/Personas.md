Persona 1 - Tom Hargreaves, 58, Arable & Livestock Farmer
Tom runs a mid-sized farm in rural Yorkshire with a herd of 200 beef cattle.
He's been farming his whole life and is skeptical of new technology, preferring to trust his own experience and instincts.
He does own a smartphone but mostly uses it for calls and occasionally checking the weather. 
His main concern during summer is heat stress in his cattle, and during winter he worries about waterlogged grazing fields
making conditions dangerous for the animals. Tom doesn't have time to sit in front of a computer — he needs information fast, 
ideally at a glance while he's out in the field. He doesn't want graphs or technical readings, 
just a clear signal telling him whether conditions are safe, borderline, or dangerous for his herd right now.

Frustrations: Overly technical interfaces, information that requires interpretation, anything that only works on a desktop.



Persona 2 - Priya Nair, 29, Agricultural Field Officer
Priya works for a regional farming advisory body and is responsible for monitoring environmental conditions across 15 different 
farms in her area. She visits farms regularly but also needs to monitor conditions remotely between visits. She's comfortable with 
technology and regularly uses data tools in her work. Her job requires her to identify farms that may be at risk and prioritise 
where to send support — for example, flagging farms with persistently high soil moisture that could indicate a disease risk to 
livestock such as liver fluke. She needs to compare conditions across multiple sites, review historical trends, and produce 
written reports for her organisation. Currently she does this manually by emailing farmers and collating spreadsheets, 
which is time-consuming and unreliable.

Frustrations: No multi-site overview, inability to filter by date or location, having to chase farmers individually for readings, 
no export functionality.

Persona 3 - Sipho Nkosi, 45, Farm Security and Community Watch Manager
Sipho oversees security for a large agricultural estate and works with local community watch groups in the East side of Cape. He
is very observant and is dependent on his smartphone to deliver a response to his team. Daily challenges include livestock theft
and trespassing into the farm, in which he needs immediate, highly reliable push alerts when the gate is opened at abnormal hours
or fences around the perimeter have been breached via vibration sensors. It is essential he gets notifications as fast as possible
if the system detects a 'flee' event, where the accelerometer has peaked, so he can track their GPS coordinates in real time to
intercept potential rustlers (livestock theives). He often operates during the night shift and needs a simple high-contrast mobile
interface that outlines critical alerts immediately that is easy to view in the dark or requiring him to navigate through multiple
screens.

Frustrations: False alarms that waste his team's time, delayed notifications, systems that do not provide accurate GPS measurements
for breaches and fleeing animals, complex interface that slows down response time. 

Persona 4 - Dr. Sarah van de Merwe, 38, Mobile Large Animal Veterinarian
Sarah is in charge of multiple farms to maintain and monitor herd health. She is very anayltical and appreciates data that aids
her practice in preventative medicine. While she is rarely at her desk, she extensively uses a tablet in her jeep between farm
visits. Her main concern is locating sick, injured, or heat-prolonged animals early. She espescially looks for activtiy that flag
as 'low activity' (lack of movement for long periods of time during daytime) which correlates to underlying illness. She wants the
opportunity to access a herd's specific 7-day activity and ambient temperatures overtime to finalise a foundation before arriving
on-site for an inspection. A neccesity is a dashboard view that enables her to filter out farm's security/gate notifications and
focus exclusively on health metrics for the animals. 

Frustrations: Finding out an animal is sick only after physical symptoms are explicit, dashboards that prioritise security events
over animal health metrics, inability to seek historical baseline data to compare against current herd behaviour.
